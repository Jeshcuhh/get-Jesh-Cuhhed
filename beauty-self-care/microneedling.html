At-Home Microneedling

A careful protocol covering required materials, depth guidance by facial zone, the step-by-step process, and the recovery boundaries that keep the routine conservative

The Premise

Controlled micro-injury · collagen response
Foundational

Here's the loveliest part: you're not damaging your skin, you're asking it to repair itself. Fine needles create controlled micro-injuries, and the skin responds with its own wound-healing capabilities, producing more collagen and elastin, the proteins responsible for firmness and elasticity. Minimal downtime. No harsh resurfacing. Just a quiet conversation with your own biology. [nih]

02

Where It Shines

Scarring · pigmentation · texture
Broad indication

This is the part I love telling people about, because the range is remarkable for something so understated. It works beautifully on atrophic acne scars, fine lines, hyperpigmentation, melasma, stretch marks, surgical and burn scars, and even androgenetic alopecia

Compared with chemical peels and laser, it often delivers higher satisfaction and a lower risk of pigmentation issues, which matters enormously for darker skin tones. It's the rare treatment that doesn't ask you to choose between results and safety. [nih]

03

The Honest Timeline

Collagen remodels over months
In 6–12 months

A note on patience, because this isn't an injectable, it doesn't perform overnight. Collagen remodels over months, gradually and on its own schedule. Most people maintain results with treatments every six to twelve months. Think of it less as a fix and more as a practice

Frequency scales with needle depth: 0–0.5 mm can be done daily; 0.5–2 mm every 4 weeks or less frequently; >2 mm every 8 weeks

04

Prep Timeline

What to pause · when to pause it
Begin 1–2 weeks out

Always monitor timelines

1–2 weeks before

  • Blood thinners: avoid NSAIDs like ibuprofen (Advil/Motrin) and aspirin, consult your prescribing doctor if you're on blood thinners for a medical condition
  • Supplements: stop fish oil, high-dose Vitamin E, and herbal supplements (St. John's Wort, Ginkgo Biloba)

3–7 days before

  • Retinoids: stop all prescription and OTC retinol or retinoid creams (Tretinoin, Adapalene)
  • Exfoliants: halt AHAs (glycolic, lactic), BHAs (salicylic acid), facial scrubs, and at-home peel pads
  • Acne treatments: pause benzoyl peroxide and spot treatments
  • Other treatments: avoid waxing, laser hair removal, or chemical peels in the target area

24–48 hours before

  • Sun exposure: avoid direct, unprotected sun and tanning beds
  • Alcohol & caffeine: limit alcohol (impedes healing, causes swelling); skip caffeine on the day of your appointment

The day of

  • Cleanse only: start with a freshly washed face (no makeup, lotion, oils, or sunscreen)
05

Required Materials

Device · serums · sanitation
One-time setup
  • Derminator 2: the device anchor for this routine [nih]
  • NeuroMed BLT Numbing Cream: applied pre-treatment to manage discomfort [nih]
  • AnteAGE® Regenerative Science Hyaluronic Acid: primary slip and post-pass leave-on [nih]
  • Cos De Baha: secondary serum option for the routine
  • Rejuran Healer Turnover Ampoule: helps improve the appearance of skin's tone, texture, and radiance [nih]
  • Sterile single-use cartridges: no reusing needles and no bargain-bin risk
  • Alcohol pads / wipes: for swabbing skin and surfaces
  • Disposable gloves: one pair, swapped if contaminated
  • Soft sponges: for removing numbing cream cleanly
  • Secure headband: keeps hair fully out of the field
  • Clean silicone brush: dedicated to serum application only
06

The Rule Set

Non-negotiables before you begin
Every session
  1. Do not microneedle over active acne, irritation, infection, sunburn, or compromised skin
  2. Never exceed 1.25 mm depth at home, regardless of area
  3. Skip aggressive actives while skin is recovering
  4. Plan around sun exposure and events. This is not a same-night-before-going-out routine
  5. Treat face, neck, and décolletage in separate sessions, never all at once
  6. When anything looks wrong, stop and get professional help instead of pushing through

08

Step-by-Step Protocol

Prep · treatment · recovery
~45 minutes
01 · Preparation
  1. Thoroughly cleanse face
  2. Apply NeuroMed BLT numbing cream in an even layer
  3. Wait 10–15 minutes for it to take effect
  4. Remove numbing cream completely with a soft sponge
  5. Cleanse face again with chlorhexidine
  6. Swab the entire treatment area with an alcohol pad
02 · Treatment
  1. Apply hyaluronic acid serum with the dedicated silicone brush
  2. Work in sections using a grid pattern: vertical, then horizontal, then diagonal passes
  3. Keep skin taut with the non-pen hand for better, more even penetration
  4. Use the shortest strokes possible and work quickly while numbing is active
  5. Watch for even pinkness (erythema) as the endpoint, stop there
  6. Reapply hyaluronic acid as needed for slip throughout the session
03 · Immediate Post-Treatment
  • Leave hyaluronic acid on the skin
  • Press in 3–5 drops of Rejuran Healer Turnover Ampoule over the HA while skin is still tacky, ideal timing for PDRN support and enhanced delivery through fresh channels. After 60+ min, seal with a ceramide moisturizer. Nothing else that night: no retinoids, acids, vitamin C, or actives
04 · Next Few Days Post-Treatment
  • Resume regular skincare the next day, kept bland
  • Wait 3 days before reintroducing retinol or tretinoin
  • Add kiwi seed oil as the last step in the routine
  • Expect 3–5 days of dry, tight skin while the barrier repairs
09

Sanitation & Sterile Field

Pen hand · gloves · serum brush
Throughout session
  • Designate one hand for the pen, never letting it touch the face
  • Keep a separate, clean silicone brush reserved for serum only
  • Sanitize hands between activities and swap gloves the moment they're compromised
  • Keep alcohol wipes within arm's reach throughout the session

Safety note: microneedling can cause infection, scarring, irritation, and pigment issues when done poorly. This suits my needs and is fully not medical advice. Adjust parameters to your skin's needs, err on the side of caution, and consult a licensed professional for personalized guidance.
