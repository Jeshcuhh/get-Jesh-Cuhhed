At-Home Press-On Acrylic

13 mini steps

  1. Sanitize & set up

    Clean hands and tools, then lay aluminum foil across your surface for fast clean-up. Lay out every product within reach before you start

  2. Sizing

    With a file, sand each side of the nail for a few seconds, until it fits on the nail, sidewall to sidewall

  3. Remove the shine

    With an e-file or a fine sanding band, lightly buff each natural nail for a few seconds on a high grit low setting. Wipe away dust with rubbing alcohol

  4. Prime

    Apply a thin layer of acrylic primer to each natural nail to help prevent lifting

  5. Prep fast-set acrylic

    Add a few drops of monomer enhancer to your monomer so the acrylic sets quickly and predictably

  6. Bead the tip

    Pick up a small acrylic bead on the brush and place it on the underside of the press-on tip and swipe the brush top to bottom to reinforce the nail

  7. Attach at 45°

    Wait a few seconds, then set the tip onto the nail at roughly a 45-degree angle and press down slowly to push out any air bubbles

  8. Hold until set

    Hold for about 10 seconds. With the monomer enhancer, the acrylic cures fully in roughly 20 seconds

  9. Reinforce the back

    Add a little extra acrylic behind the tip for a stronger hold, especially for medium or long lengths

  10. Refine the shape

    Use a hand file and the e-file with the Ceramic Tip to remove any excess material and clean up the edges

  11. Top coatOptional

    Apply a gel top coat for extra durability and cure for 60 seconds

  12. Repeat across the set

    Work nail by nail. A finished set takes about 30–50 minutes and lasts up to three weeks
