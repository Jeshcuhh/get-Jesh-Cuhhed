Dermaplaner

A mechanical exfoliation note for glow, smoother product application, and using less foundation without pretending a blade is risk-free

Archive

Required Materials

  • Leaf shave Dermaplaner
  • A gentle cleanser
  • 70% rubbing alcohol
  • Squalene oil

Why it Works

  • Removes dead skin and fine facial hair so light reflects more evenly
  • Creates a smoother surface for skincare and makeup
  • Can help foundation sit thinner and cleaner, which means using less product
  • The Leaf shave approach has a long break-even horizon, but it reduces disposable plastic over time

The Boundaries

  1. Avoid active acne, inflamed breakouts, irritation, or compromised skin
  2. Use a clean blade and do not rush around delicate areas
  3. Follow with bland hydration and sunscreen
  4. Do not stack with aggressive exfoliation the same day
  5. Repeat roughly every 3–4 weeks if skin tolerates it
Safety note: this page is educational/personal content. Dermaplaning can irritate skin or spread bacteria when done over active breakouts; make the right judgement or contact a dermatology

Where the dollar goes

A blade, a clean surface, and a few minutes is the entire procedure: the rest of a dermaplaning facial's price is the room, the brand, and the time you pay someone else for. With a Leaf razor at home, the cost rounds to almost nothing. At the spa, only a thin slice of every dollar is the service actually touching your skin.
