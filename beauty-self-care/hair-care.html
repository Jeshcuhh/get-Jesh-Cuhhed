Hair Care

The in-between work: protect tone, keep ends soft, and avoid panic-damage from over-correcting

01

The Principles

Tone protection · structural health · patience
Foundational
  • Hair care starts at the scalp
  • Treat wash days as maintenance, not a huge change every month
  • Use bond repair after chemical work and give hair downtime between processes
  • Keep heat deliberate: lower temperature, fewer passes, and heat protectant
  • Brush from ends upward and keep friction low while sleeping with silk pillowcases
  • Light 0.5–1 cm dusting/cut every 6 months, point cut
02

The Rule Set

Non-negotiables for every wash and style
Every session
  1. Wash when needed, double shampoo as needed
  2. Condition mids and ends first; do not overload the scalp
  3. Never skip heat protectant, Chi 44 Iron Guard before any hot tool
  4. Schedule toner refreshes instead of drying purple shampoos
  5. Expensive-looking hair is usually healthy-looking hair.
03

Required Materials

Shampoo · treatments · styling
Full kit
Wash & Treatment
  • FHI Heat UNbrush Detangling Hair Brush: hardly any hair snagged when brushed
  • L'Oreal EverPure Bond Repair Shampoo/Conditioner: daily driver for color-treated hair
  • Clarifying shampoo (double-wash): strip buildup without stripping tone (any shampoo from Costco)
  • ABC Deep Care Treatment: deep repair mask
  • BlondMe Cool Blondes Mask: toning and hydration mask
  • K18 Leave-In Molecular Repair Mask: bond repair, a holy grail
  • Minoxidil: Gold standard for hair regrowth
  • Ketoconazole: Dermatologist's treatment for dandruff and hair loss prevention
Leave-In & Styling
  • Oribe Leave-In Conditioner: lightweight detangle and moisture layer
  • Pureology Hairspray: flexible hold without crunch
  • Garnier Fructis Sleek & Shine Anti-Frizz Serum: smoothing finish on ends
  • Chi 44 Iron Guard Heat Protectant: applied before any heat styling, non-negotiable

05

The Wash Routine

Cleanse · treat · condition · finish
~30 minutes
01 · Cleanse
  1. Wet hair thoroughly with lukewarm water
  2. First shampoo with L'Oreal EverPure Bond Repair: dime size amount, evenly distributed on the palms and applied on the scalp, massage gently, rinse
  3. On clarifying days (2× per week): double shampoo: repeat with a clarifying wash to strip buildup
02 · Treat (rotate weekly)
  • Starting off: Use 4 to 6 consecutive washes initially. Afterward, switch to a maintenance phase of once every 3 to 4 washes apply K-18 hairmask from mids to ends, leave on
    5–10 minutes,
  • Other Wash Days: apply ABC Deep Care Treatment from mids to ends, leave 5–10 minutes, dermaplane or whitten teeth while waiting, rinse, apply Blondme Cooling mask for 2 mins or more
03 · Condition
  1. Apply L'Oreal EverPure conditioner to mids and ends only
  2. Detangle gently with fingers or a wide-tooth comb
  3. Rinse with cool water to close the cuticle
04 · Leave-In & Style
  1. Towel-blot (don't rub) excess water
  2. Color Fanatic Heat Protectant Leave-In Conditioner
  3. Apply Oribe leave-in conditioner through lengths
  4. Work Garnier Fructis anti-frizz serum into ends
  5. If heat styling: apply Chi 44 Iron Guard before any hot tool
  6. Dry roots first, leave lengths gentler when possible
  7. Finish with Pureology hairspray for flexible hold
06

Heat & Styling

Temperature · technique · protection
As needed
  • Always apply Chi 44 Iron Guard before heat
  • Use the lowest effective temperature. Color-treated hair rarely needs above 350°F
  • Fewer passes are better than more. Get sections right the first time
  • Dry roots with heat; let lengths air-dry when time allows
  • Brush from ends upward to avoid snapping strands
  • Sleep on silk or satin to reduce friction and preserve style
07

Trim & Maintenance

Dusting · toning · recovery
Every 6 months
  • Light 0.5–1 cm dusting every 6 months, point cut to preserve texture
  • Schedule tone refreshes: plan them, don't panic-buy purple shampoo
  • After any chemical service (toning, coloring): use K18 or bond repair and give hair downtime before the next process
  • Monitor ends. If they feel rough or start splitting, move the trim up
