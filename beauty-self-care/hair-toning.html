Hair Toning

A demi-permanent gloss routine for keeping tone cool, dimensional, and soft without over-processing.

The Supplies

  • Shades EQ 6T (Iron): root tap
  • Shades EQ 8T (Silver): mids and ends
  • Shades EQ 9P (Opal Glow): mids and ends
  • EQ Processing Solution: 1:2 ratio with color
  • Olaplex No. 1: a few drops per mix
  • K18: post-rinse finishing mask
  • Mixing bowl, applicator brush, gloves, and timer

The Method

  1. Start on towel-blotted wet hair
  2. Root tap: 15 ml 6T + 30 ml Processing Solution + one drop Olaplex No. 1. Apply 1–1.5 inches from scalp
  3. Mids and ends: equal parts 8T and 9P + Processing Solution at 1:2 + a few drops Olaplex No. 1
  4. Apply from where the root tap ends through the ends. Saturate fully
  5. Process about 20 minutes. Step away: go exercise
  6. Rinse thoroughly until the water runs clear
  7. Finish with a K18 treatment
Technique note: Shades EQ deposits tone without lift, so the approach is gentler than lightening. The 6T root tap keeps depth at the scalp natural; the 8T/9P blend creates the cool, dimensional finish.

By Measure

Where the dollar goes

A demi-permanent gloss is one of the easiest salon services to learn: and one of the most marked-up. In the Bay Area a root tap and toner starts at $100 before tip; once you can mix and time it yourself, that $120 ticket becomes a twenty-minute habit. At the salon, only a sliver of every dollar actually deposits onto your hair.

"Consistency is elegance. Learning is leverage. The most expensive look is the one you can repeat at home."
