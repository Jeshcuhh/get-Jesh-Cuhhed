Jewelry

A gemstone tier list where hardness is not the same as toughness, and durability matters more than hype

Metals Tier List

heavily prioritizes longevity, repairability, and engineering considerations

S tier
Platinum alloys, 18k yellow gold, Argentium silver, 18k palladium white gold: maximum purity, exceptional tarnish resistance, and ideal for lifetime daily wear
A tier
Palladium white gold, 22k yellow gold - 14k yellow gold: fantastic everyday durability, rich classic tones, and highly practical workhorse alloys
B tier
Sterling silver, Green gold (Electrum): beautiful traditional luster, though they require routine polishing or specialized care to handle oxidation
C tier
Titanium, Stainless steel, Copper, Brass, 24k gold, Fine silver, Purple gold, Blue gold: mixed utility; either highly durable but difficult to resize, or beautiful niche colors that are too soft for high-wear jewelry
D tier
Rhodium-plated white gold: crisp, bright finish, but requires ongoing re-plating maintenance as the base metal begins to show through
E tier
Tungsten carbide: incredibly scratch-resistant, but brittle enough to shatter on high impact and entirely impossible to resize
F tier
Gold-plated base metal jewelry, Gold vermeil: budget-friendly appeal, but the thin gold layer wears away quickly under daily friction, exposing the base metal beneath

Gemstone Tier List

Hardness means scratch resistance. Toughness means resistance to breaking. A stone can be hard and still cleave, chip, or become annoying in daily wear

Buy for the life you actually live: how often you wear it, what it knocks against, and how much upkeep you will tolerate

God tier
Corundum: ruby and sapphire, Mohs 9, no cleavage, extremely tough, every color, keeps polish forever
S tier
Moissanite, lab diamond, spinel: durable, brilliant, and often smarter value than natural diamond hype
A tier
Garnet, chrysoberyl: useful durability, beautiful color, and good everyday logic depending on setting
B tier
Tourmaline, cubic zirconia, demantoid garnet, nephrite jade, jadeite: pretty, but know the maintenance tradeoffs
C tier
Aquamarine, topaz, quartz family, natural diamond: mixed durability/value notes
D tier
Morganite, peridot: can be beautiful but show wear or grime faster
E/F tier
Emerald, tanzanite, synthetic rutile, pearl, opal, feldspar family: high romance, high caution
